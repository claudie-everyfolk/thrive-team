# Contract Review Playbook — Vendor SaaS Agreements

**Version:** 4.1
**Owner:** Legal & Compliance
**Scope:** All inbound vendor SaaS / data processing agreements ≥ $25K ACV or any deal involving member or employee data, regardless of value.

This playbook defines acceptable positions ("**In policy**"), positions that require negotiation but can sometimes be lived with ("**Soft deviation**"), and positions we will not accept without VP Legal sign-off ("**Hard deviation – escalate**"). Use the most restrictive applicable column.

---

## 1. Term and Renewal

| Position | Status |
|---|---|
| Initial term ≤ 24 months | In policy |
| Initial term 25-36 months | Soft deviation — push back to 24 |
| Initial term > 36 months | Hard deviation — escalate |
| Auto-renew ≤ 12 months with ≥ 60 days notice window | In policy |
| Auto-renew > 12 months OR notice window > 90 days | Hard deviation — escalate |
| Renewal price increase capped at CPI or 7%, whichever is lower | In policy |
| Renewal increase 7-10% with cap | Soft deviation |
| Renewal increase > 10% or uncapped | Hard deviation — escalate |

## 2. Fees and Payment

- **Net 45** payment terms is our standard. Net 30 acceptable. Anything shorter (Net 15, due on receipt) is a **soft deviation** — negotiate to Net 30 minimum.
- Late interest > 1.5% / month is a **soft deviation**.
- Any clause that deems invoices "accepted" if not disputed within < 30 days is a **hard deviation** — our AP cycle alone runs longer than that.

## 3. Intellectual Property

- We retain ownership of all Customer Data and any derivative works of Customer Data. **In policy.**
- Vendor-owned IP for the underlying service. **In policy.**
- Any clause that assigns Feedback, suggestions, or improvements from Customer to Vendor with no restriction is a **soft deviation** — we'll accept a non-exclusive license to use Feedback, but no assignment.
- Any clause asserting vendor ownership over Customer Data, even in part, is a **hard deviation — escalate**.

## 4. Customer Data and Data Processing

This is our most-redlined section. Tighten everything.

- **Use of Customer Data for vendor's own purposes** (including aggregated / de-identified data for model training, benchmarking, product improvement): **Hard deviation — escalate.** Our default position: vendor may use Customer Data only to provide the Service to us. No training. No benchmarking against other customers. No "product improvement" loophole.
- Breach notification > 48 hours: **Hard deviation** for any deal involving member or employee PII. Soft deviation otherwise.
- "Reasonable" or "industry-standard" security with no specifics: **Soft deviation** — require attachment of a security exhibit or DPA referencing specific standards.
- Security practices that can be "updated from time to time" without notice: **Soft deviation** — require 30 days notice and a right to terminate if materially weakened.
- SOC 2 Type II or equivalent (ISO 27001): required. Anything less: **Hard deviation**.
- DPA must be attached and signed at execution for any deal touching PII. Not "available upon request." **Hard deviation** if not attached.

## 5. Indemnification

- Vendor indemnifies us for IP infringement claims related to the Service. **In policy.**
- Standard carve-outs (combination claims, modifications, post-notice use) are acceptable. **In policy.**
- Customer indemnifies vendor for our use of the Service, our data, and breach: **Soft deviation** — narrow to gross negligence, willful misconduct, and our breach of the license restrictions. No general "any and all claims."
- Mutual data-breach indemnity is **required** for any deal touching member or employee PII. Absence is a **hard deviation**.

## 6. Limitation of Liability

| Position | Status |
|---|---|
| Cap = 12 months fees | In policy |
| Cap = 6-11 months fees | Soft deviation — push to 12 |
| Cap < 6 months fees | Hard deviation — escalate |
| Mutual cap with carve-outs for confidentiality, IP indemnity, data breach, gross negligence | Required |
| Carve-outs only for payment obligations | Hard deviation — must add data breach carve-out at minimum |
| Disclaimer of consequential damages (mutual) | In policy |

For deals touching member or employee data, **super-cap for data breach = 2x annual fees minimum**.

## 7. Most Favored Customer / Most Favored Nation

We do not accept MFN clauses imposed on us. If vendor wants MFN, **hard deviation** — refuse.

We also do not grant MFN to vendors — but a vendor representing they're giving *us* MFN is a different story. Treat vendor-side MFN representations as **soft deviation** — they're usually unenforceable theater, but strike them anyway to avoid confusion at renewal.

## 8. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

- Governing law: **Delaware, New York, or California** — **in policy**. Anywhere else: **soft deviation**.
- Venue / forum: must align with governing law. Vendor's home state OK if it matches their governing law election.
- Mandatory binding arbitration with class action waiver: **soft deviation** — we generally prefer courts, but accept arbitration if the forum is reasonable and discovery rights are preserved. JAMS or AAA acceptable.
- Loser-pays / fee-shifting: **soft deviation** — strike unless mutual.
- Jury trial waiver: in policy.

## 9. Termination

- Termination for material breach with 30-60 day cure period: **in policy**.
- Termination for convenience by us with notice: nice to have, not required.
- Data return / export on termination: must be free, in a usable format, with a minimum 30-day window. Any data return fee is a **soft deviation**. Any "non-refundable under any circumstances including vendor breach" clause is a **hard deviation** — refunds must be available for termination caused by vendor's material breach.

## 10. General / Boilerplate

- Unilateral right for vendor to modify terms via website posting: **hard deviation — escalate**. Always strike. All modifications must be in writing and signed by both parties.
- Assignment without consent: **soft deviation** unless vendor carves out affiliates and successors in a merger / acquisition.
- Entire agreement, severability, no-waiver: standard, in policy.

---

## Triage decision rule

- **All sections in policy** → fast-track, internal sign-off only.
- **Any soft deviations only** → negotiate via email, target 1 redline cycle.
- **Any hard deviation** → escalate to VP Legal before responding to vendor. Do not signal flexibility on hard deviations in initial response.
