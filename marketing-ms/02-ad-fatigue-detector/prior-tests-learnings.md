# Prior Tests — What We've Learned (rolling notes)

Last updated 2026-05-17. Keep this short — only what's actually moved decisions.

## What's worked

- **Creator-led UGC + problem-solution angle** has consistently outperformed studio in prospecting since February. Best variants land CPA 20–30% below account average in weeks 1–2.
- **"How is this only $X" style hooks** (price-anchored question) outperformed generic price callouts. The question framing matters — drives higher CTR and equivalent CVR.
- **Static stat-shock cards** punch above their weight at low cost when refreshed every 3 weeks. They die fast (~3 weeks) but cheap to produce.
- **Pattern-interrupt opens** (creator mid-action, no intro) consistently beat traditional "Hi, I'm X" intros across formats.

## What's failed

- **Aspirational studio-led** (CR-2402, CR-2406, CR-2410 lineage): every variant we've run in this lane has decayed inside 30 days and consistently lands above account-avg CPA. Stop greenlighting these unless there's a brand reason.
- **Long-form (>30 sec) creator storytime**: CTR was fine but CVR cratered. Members watched but didn't click through.
- **Generic before-after motion graphics** (CR-2404, CR-2409 lineage): keep producing acceptable CTR but ROAS underperforms by ~30%. Audience treats them as "just another ad."
- **Influencer mega-creators (>1M followers)**: poor CPA in prospecting; works for retention only.

## Things we want to try but haven't

- A **kitchen-counter testimonial** format from a *new* creator vertical we haven't tested — fitness or busy-parent. (Note: we just launched @busymom.fitness in CR-2407 — this counts as the first test in that vertical, watch closely.)
- A **price-anchored question hook** but in a **static** format instead of UGC — would isolate whether it's the hook or the format doing the work.
- **Time-of-day cutdown**: a 10–12 second version of our best UGC variants for top-of-feed placements.
- **Member voiceover over UGC b-roll** — synthetic hybrid that hasn't been tested in this account.

## Open hypotheses for the next test cycle

- If creator-led UGC + problem-solution keeps winning, the next test isn't "more of the same" — it's varying the *creator vertical* (parent / fitness / professional) while holding hook + angle constant. Isolate which audience identity converts best.
- Hook fatigue may be more about the *creator face* than the *hook text*. Worth running an A/B with the same hook copy across two creators.
