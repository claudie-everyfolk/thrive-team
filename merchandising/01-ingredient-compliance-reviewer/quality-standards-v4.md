# Owned & Partner Brand Quality Standards — v4.2

Effective: 2026-01-15
Owner: FSQA — Ingredient Standards

These standards govern every SKU sold under owned brand and every new vendor submission in the natural / better-for-you grocery categories. Any ingredient outside this guidance requires written FSQA exception.

## 1. Restricted ingredient framework

We use three statuses:

- **Acceptable** — meets standard, no extra documentation required.
- **Restricted (docs required)** — usable only if vendor supplies supporting documentation (origin letter, spec sheet, third-party test). Held in pending status until docs are on file.
- **Unacceptable** — never permitted. Submission must be reformulated or rejected.

The authoritative master list lives in `restricted-ingredients-master.csv`. The notes below clarify edge cases.

## 2. Sweeteners

- Cane sugar, maple syrup, honey, coconut sugar: Acceptable.
- Brown rice syrup: **Restricted** — vendor must supply a recent (within 12 months) third-party arsenic test result. Inorganic arsenic must be <100 ppb.
- High-fructose corn syrup, sucralose, aspartame, acesulfame potassium, saccharin: **Unacceptable**.
- Stevia, monk fruit: Acceptable when the only co-ingredient is a natural carrier (erythritol carrier requires documentation).

## 3. Colors

- Any natural color from a named botanical source (annatto, turmeric, beet juice, spirulina): Acceptable.
- **Caramel color**: **Restricted** — only Class I (plain caramel) is permitted. Vendor must supply a specification sheet confirming Class I. Class III and Class IV caramel are **Unacceptable** due to 4-MeI concerns.
- FD&C synthetic dyes (Red 40, Yellow 5, Yellow 6, Blue 1, Blue 2, Green 3): **Unacceptable**.

## 4. Preservatives

- Mixed tocopherols, rosemary extract, ascorbic acid: Acceptable.
- Potassium sorbate, sodium benzoate: **Restricted** — only permitted in refrigerated and high-moisture products (aw > 0.85). Shelf-stable snacks must reformulate.
- BHA, BHT, TBHQ, propyl gallate: **Unacceptable**.

## 5. Flavors and extracts

- "Natural flavor" as an ingredient: **Restricted** — vendor must provide a sourcing letter confirming no synthetic solvents and no undeclared allergens (especially nut, dairy, gluten).
- "Artificial flavor": **Unacceptable**.

## 6. Emulsifiers and texture agents

- Soy lecithin, sunflower lecithin: Acceptable. Soy lecithin triggers a soy allergen declaration check.
- Mono- and diglycerides: **Restricted** — vendor must confirm non-hydrogenated and supply source (palm vs soy vs other) for label transparency.
- Polysorbate 60/80, carrageenan (in beverages or dairy alternatives): **Restricted** — requires FSQA review.

## 7. Gluten and allergen claims

Any product carrying a gluten-free claim on pack requires either a third-party gluten test (<20 ppm) or a credentialed gluten-free certification (GFCO, NSF). Vendor self-attestation is not sufficient.

## 8. Approval routing

- All Acceptable + no Restricted items → Category Manager may approve.
- Any Restricted item → status is "needs-FSQA" until documents arrive and FSQA signs off.
- Any Unacceptable item → reject and request reformulation. Do not advance to FSQA.

## 9. Vendor communication norms

When requesting documentation, batch the asks into a single email per submission. Reference the specific ingredient and the document required. Set a 10-business-day clock; submissions stale beyond 30 days are returned to vendor.
